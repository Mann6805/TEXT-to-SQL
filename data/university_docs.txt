TABLE: Students
COLUMNS:
- id (INTEGER, primary key)
- name (TEXT)
- email (TEXT)
- department (TEXT)
- year (INTEGER)
- gpa (REAL)

EXAMPLES:
(1, "Rahul Sharma", "rahul@example.com", "Computer Science", 3, 8.2)
(2, "Priya Singh", "priya@example.com", "Mechanical Engineering", 2, 7.8)
(3, "Aman Verma", "aman@example.com", "Electrical Engineering", 4, 8.9)
(4, "Neha Gupta", "neha@example.com", "Computer Science", 1, 9.1)
(5, "Sanjay Patel", "sanjay@example.com", "Computer Science", 2, 8.5)

NATURAL LANGUAGE:
- Contains student details with GPA, departments, and academic year.
- Useful for queries about performance, GPA, departments, and student names.



TABLE: Professors
COLUMNS:
- id (INTEGER, primary key)
- name (TEXT)
- department (TEXT)

EXAMPLES:
(1, "Dr. Arvind Kumar", "Computer Science")
(2, "Dr. Meera Nair", "Mechanical Engineering")
(3, "Dr. Sunil Rao", "Physics")

NATURAL LANGUAGE:
- Contains list of professors with departments.



TABLE: Courses
COLUMNS:
- id (INTEGER, primary key)
- name (TEXT)
- department (TEXT)
- credits (INTEGER)

EXAMPLES:
(1, "Data Structures", "Computer Science", 4)
(2, "Thermodynamics", "Mechanical Engineering", 3)
(3, "Machine Learning", "Computer Science", 4)
(4, "Digital Circuits", "Electrical Engineering", 3)

NATURAL LANGUAGE:
- Contains course names, their departments, and credits.



TABLE: Departments
COLUMNS:
- id (INTEGER, primary key)
- name (TEXT)
- building (TEXT)

EXAMPLES:
(1, "Computer Science", "Block A")

NATURAL LANGUAGE:
- Contains university department names and their buildings.



TABLE: Enrollments
COLUMNS:
- id (INTEGER, primary key)
- student_id (INTEGER)
- course_id (INTEGER)
- semester (TEXT)
- grade (TEXT)

EXAMPLES:
(1, 1, 1, "Fall 2024", "A")

RELATIONSHIPS:
- Students.id → Enrollments.student_id
- Courses.id → Enrollments.course_id

NATURAL LANGUAGE:
- Represents which student is enrolled in which course.
- Use for joining Students and Courses.
